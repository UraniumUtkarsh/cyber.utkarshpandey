# Why Public WiFi is Dangerous 

(Edited on April 1st, 2026)

Public WiFi is widely available in cafes, airports, malls, and public spaces. While it offers convenience and free internet access, it also introduces significant security risks that many users overlook.

This article explains why public WiFi is dangerous and how you can protect yourself.

---

## 1. Untrusted Network Ownership

When you connect to public WiFi, you have no control over who owns or manages the network.

Attackers can create fake WiFi networks with names such as:
- Free Airport WiFi
- Cafe Guest

Once connected, your internet traffic may pass through the attacker’s system.

This type of attack is known as a **Rogue Access Point attack**.

---

## 2. Man-in-the-Middle (MITM) Attacks

Public WiFi networks often lack proper encryption, making them vulnerable to interception.

In a **Man-in-the-Middle (MITM) attack**, an attacker can:
- Intercept your data
- Read sensitive information
- Capture login credentials
- Modify transmitted data

---

## 3. Session Hijacking

Websites use session cookies to keep users logged in.

Attackers can steal these cookies and gain unauthorized access to:
- Social media accounts
- Email accounts
- Active sessions

This can happen without needing your password.

---

## 4. Malware Injection

Attackers can inject malicious code into:
- Websites
- Downloads
- Advertisements

This may result in:
- Spyware installation
- Keyloggers
- Unauthorized system access

---

## 5. Packet Sniffing

Using network analysis tools, attackers can monitor:
- Websites you visit
- Data packets transmitted over the network
- Unencrypted communications

This technique is known as **packet sniffing**.

---

## 6. Financial and Identity Theft

Accessing sensitive services over public WiFi increases the risk of:
- Credit or debit card theft
- Account compromise
- Identity theft

This is especially risky when using:
- Online banking
- Payment gateways
- Personal accounts

---

## How to Stay Safe

Follow these best practices to reduce risk:

### Use a VPN
A Virtual Private Network encrypts your internet traffic and protects your data.

### Avoid Sensitive Transactions
Do not access banking, enter passwords, or share confidential data on public WiFi.

### Use HTTPS Websites
Ensure the website uses HTTPS (look for the padlock icon in the browser).

### Disable Auto-Connect
Prevent your device from automatically connecting to unknown networks.

### Prefer Mobile Data
Use cellular data for sensitive tasks instead of public WiFi.

---

## Conclusion

Public WiFi networks are convenient but inherently insecure.

If you do not control the network, you should not trust it with sensitive information.

Understanding these risks and following basic security practices can significantly reduce your exposure to cyber threats.
